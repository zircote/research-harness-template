This exec-summary synthesis covers 36 surviving finding(s) across the research.

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

Yes — layer MIF as the modeling, provenance, and temporal spine beneath OKF as the git-distributable packaging surface. The research clears all four decision bars: the layering is technically feasible, MIF is differentiated from existing knowledge-representation prior art, the adoption trajectory favors structured and provenance-backed knowledge, and documented buyer segments carry the pains the spine addresses. Act now — build a thin reference implementation that emits OKF-valid bundles carrying MIF frontmatter extensions, and validate it against one provenance-heavy workflow before funding a productized governance layer. The decision turns on a single open risk: Google Cloud shipped OKF only weeks ago (12 June 2026) with no ecosystem, and its very minimalism may be what buyers want — so the pilot must prove buyers will pay for MIF's richer layer, not merely adopt OKF's simpler one.

## Key Findings

Feasibility: OKF v0.1 lets producers add any frontmatter keys and requires consumers to preserve unknown keys, so MIF's typed relationships, provenance, temporal, and ontology fields inject cleanly as OKF frontmatter extensions. The one real tension is OKF's permissive-consumer model against MIF's fail-closed validation — a design choice to resolve, not a blocker.

Differentiation: OKF v0.1 types every inter-concept link only in surrounding prose and records a single last-changed timestamp. MIF adds typed, directed relationship edges with optional strength (0-1), a first-class W3C PROV-O-compatible provenance object (source type, confidence, trust level, attribution chain), and bi-temporal tracking with configurable decay — replacing OKF's informal conventions with a machine-verifiable record.

Trajectory: Hybrid graph-plus-vector retrieval now beats pure vector RAG by 3.4x on accuracy and clears 90%+ on schema-bound queries [3]; the enterprise knowledge-graph market compounds at 21-36% annually, with Gartner projecting that more than half of AI-agent systems will run on context graphs by 2028 [4]; and 2026 agent-memory research names staleness and provenance as the two hardest unsolved problems — the precise gap MIF closes [5].

Prior art: The Semantic Web stalled by prioritizing logical completeness (RDF, OWL, SPARQL) over usability while minimal Schema.org won — and an OKF+MIF spine deliberately follows that pragmatic path. RDF/OWL, JSON-LD, SKOS, and Frictionless Data each cover only part of the spine (formal ontology, linked data, taxonomy, or dataset packaging); none combine git-distributable packaging with citation-backed findings, typed edges, and bi-temporal provenance.

Market: AI/ML, knowledge-engineering, research, think-tank, and developer-platform teams each carry documented provenance, LLM-grounding, or institutional-memory pain that the spine addresses. The demand direction is real, but several supporting market statistics are weakened (see Risks & Caveats) and should be read as directional, not load-bearing.

## Recommendation

> Build a thin OKF+MIF reference implementation and validate it against one provenance-heavy research workflow before funding a commercial governance layer.

What: Ship a producer/consumer pair that emits OKF-valid v0.1 bundles whose frontmatter carries MIF extensions — typed relationships, the provenance object, and bi-temporal fields — plus a validator that enforces MIF's fail-closed rules over OKF's permissive base.

Why: Every technical and differentiation bar passes and the demand trajectory is favorable, so the binding constraint is not feasibility. It is proving a buyer will pay for the richer layer rather than settle for OKF alone.

How: Pick one workflow where provenance and staleness already hurt — research synthesis or agent memory — instrument it end to end, and measure whether MIF's typed, provenance, and temporal layer changes outcomes a user will pay for.

Risk: If the pilot shows OKF's minimalism already satisfies the workflow, treat MIF as an optional enrichment profile rather than a productized layer, and defer any governance or hosting business until a second segment confirms willingness to pay.

## Risks & Caveats

OKF nascency is the dominant risk. Google Cloud published OKF v0.1 on 12 June 2026, only days before this research; the repository drew 5,440 stars and 416 forks quickly [1] but ships with no producer libraries, consumer integrations, governance, or enterprise adoption record. Demand for OKF specifically — as distinct from structured knowledge formats generally — remains undemonstrated, and OKF's minimalism may itself be the buyer preference, which would leave MIF's richer layer a solution ahead of its segment.

MIF's gap is distribution, not maturity. MIF has been public since February 2026 and reached v1.0.0 (Released, stabilized 18 June 2026) [2], so it predates OKF v0.1 and is the more stabilized specification. Its challenge is building an independent adopter base and formal governance against OKF's Google-backed launch — not closing a spec-maturity gap.

Five claims carry caveats (confidence basis).
